What is dispersed camping all about?

Dispersed camping refers to camping on available public land, further away from recreational facilities and luxury amenities that campers can otherwise pay for on public campgrounds. It has been seeing a resurgence in interest from nature enthusiasts and hobbyists alike.






What are the rules/laws?

Not all public land is accessible to free camping. Some areas may require a fire permit, have seasonal restrictions, or prohibit camping altogether to protect important habitats. One federal guideline from the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) land is that you may only spend 14 days out of a calendar month in an area which could be spent consecutively or non-consecutively. It is best to contact nearest Ranger Station before setting up camp...





What can be expected?

Each campsite will vary from the next with offering different “amenities.” Some dispersed camping sites offer nearby restrooms and can maintain decent cellular coverage… others might require more concessions. With our app, you can plan accordingly and pick a campsite that suits your desired level of comfort.




Why choose to disperse camp?

Dispersed camping is ideal for privacy, simplicity, and costs. It maximizes the escape that many seek when they picture camping in their fantasies. Why would you prepare for a weekend of roughing it, only to find yourself in earshot of others in public campgrounds that you pay for?









Things to consider:

  • Bring a paper map or know your way to the nearest ranger station.
  • Understand fire rules and restrictions.
  • If parking further from your setup, do not leave anything of value in your vehicle.
  • Respect the campsite and clean up after yourself. Any “business” needs to be buried and at least 100 feet away.
  • Tell others where you plan on staying and how long.
  • Enjoy the beauty and solitude!
